UK Building Society History

An interactive reference mapping the history of mergers, acquisitions, and renamings among UK building societies — from small Victorian benefit societies through to the major mutuals of today.

Each node represents a building society. Follow the arrows to trace how hundreds of local societies were gradually absorbed into the institutions that exist today. Click any node to see its details, or use the group filters to focus on a particular family of societies.

Data is drawn primarily from the Building Societies Association's alphabetical list of changes since 1937, cross-referenced with the FCA Mutuals Public Register and the British Newspaper Archive. This is an ongoing personal research project — coverage will grow over time.

Within building societies, acquisitions represent transfers of engagements — the specific legal mechanism by which one society absorbs another's membership and assets. See the progress page for more on what's covered and what's coming.

How to read the diagram

Star — society still exists today
Circle — renamed into another society
Diamond — merged with another society
Rounded square — acquired by another society
Hexagon — demutualised
Cut rectangle — partial transfer
Dashed — renaming
Blue — merger
Terracotta — acquisition
Gold — demutualisation
Purple — partial transfer
In Heritage view, node size and opacity reflect how many steps a society is from a current building society.
